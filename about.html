About Us

Bajirao Welfare Trust is a volunteer-driven organisation working across education, health, and community development. We organise regular after-school activities and fun learning events for children, run periodic medical camps and vaccination drives, and coordinate vocational training for youths and adults to build local skills.

During emergencies and seasonal crises — such as monsoon floods — the trust mobilises volunteers to distribute food, clean drinking water, blankets, and essential medicines to affected families. We also collect and distribute donated medicines and hygiene supplies year-round to support those who cannot afford healthcare.

Our work is delivered through local partnerships with schools, healthcare providers, and community leaders. Volunteers lead outreach, logistics, and event programming, while donor support funds medicines, learning materials, and relief supplies so we can respond quickly and sustainably to community needs.

Our Story

We began as a small group of volunteers supporting local children and families. Over the years we expanded programs in schools, organised health camps, and partnered with local stakeholders to scale our efforts. The trust is run by a committee of community members rather than a single founder.

Our Values

  • Compassion: We approach our work with empathy and a genuine desire to help.
  • Integrity: We are committed to transparency and accountability in all our actions.
  • Empowerment: We believe in giving people the tools and confidence to create their own success.
  • Community: We foster a sense of belonging and collaboration to create a stronger, more resilient society.
